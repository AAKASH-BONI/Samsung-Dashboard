# 📊 Samsung - Power BI Dashboard Project

![](https://github.com/AAKASH-BONI/Samsung-Dashboard/blob/main/1687677799775.png).

## Overview

This project focuses on analyzing the supply chain operations of Samsung Electronics using Power BI. The goal was to transform raw operational data into an interactive dashboard that helps monitor supplier performance, inventory levels, shipment activities, and overall revenue insights.

The dashboard provides a data-driven view of the supply chain, allowing users to quickly identify delays, monitor inventory stock, analyze supplier lead times, and track product performance. By visualizing key metrics and trends, the project demonstrates how business intelligence tools can support faster decision-making and operational efficiency in large global organizations like Samsung.

## Objectives

- To analyze the supply chain data of Samsung Electronics using Power BI.
- To convert raw supply chain data into meaningful and actionable insights through data visualization.
- To monitor supplier performance, lead time, and order quantity for better supplier management.
- To track inventory levels, stock availability, and reorder points to improve inventory control.
- To analyze shipment quantities and delivery delays to identify logistics inefficiencies.
- To evaluate revenue performance across different sales platforms.
- To identify product defect rates and quality issues for better operational decisions.
- To build an interactive dashboard that helps stakeholders make data-driven decisions quickly.

## Dataset

The dataset used in this project is confidential and cannot be shared publicly. It reflects a realistic HR data structure including employee demographics, compensation, and attrition metrics for analytical purposes.

## Key Features

- Interactive Dashboard: Developed an interactive Power BI dashboard to visualize supply chain operations of Samsung Electronics in a clear and user-friendly way.
- Supplier Performance Analysis: Analyzed supplier lead time, order quantities, and performance metrics to evaluate supplier efficiency.
- Inventory Management: Monitored stock levels, safety stock, and reorder points to track inventory availability.
- Shipment & Logistics Tracking: Examined shipment quantities and carrier delays to identify logistics performance and delivery issues.
- Revenue Analysis: Analyzed total revenue and platform-wise revenue distribution to understand sales performance.
- Defect Rate Monitoring: Tracked defective products and defect rates to evaluate product quality.
- Data Visualization: Used charts, KPIs, and filters to transform raw data into meaningful insights.
- Decision Support: Enabled stakeholders to quickly identify trends, inefficiencies, and opportunities for improving supply chain operations.

## Business Impact

- Improved Decision-Making: The dashboard helps stakeholders quickly analyze supply chain data and make faster, data-driven decisions for operations at Samsung    Electronics.
- Better Supplier Management: Insights into supplier lead times and performance help identify reliable suppliers and improve procurement strategies.
- Efficient Inventory Control: Monitoring stock levels and reorder points helps reduce stock shortages and overstock situations.
- Enhanced Logistics Efficiency: Shipment tracking and delay analysis help identify logistics bottlenecks and improve delivery performance.
- Revenue Performance Insights: Platform-wise revenue analysis helps understand which sales channels contribute most to business growth.
- Quality Improvement: Defect rate monitoring helps identify product quality issues and supports better quality control.
- Operational Transparency: The interactive dashboard provides a clear overview of the entire supply chain, improving overall operational visibility.

## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Power BI can be used to transform complex supply chain data into meaningful insights. By analyzing supplier performance, inventory levels, shipment activities, and revenue trends, the dashboard provides a clear view of supply chain operations at Samsung Electronics. It helps identify inefficiencies and supports faster, data-driven decision-making to improve overall operational efficiency.
